Welcome!

I'm a student in archives management at the Simmons School of Library and Information Science. This website was created as part of a project for my 488 online summer course. Use the left site menu to learn more about me, my education, academic interests, and my professional experience, explore tutorials I've developed as part of my 488 course with my classmate, Carly Moniz, and view my artwork. You can read about my goals for pursuing a career in Library and Information Science below. Thanks for visiting!

Goals for LIS:

There is so much to learn in the field of library and information science, especially as it applies to archives. The goals I have for my graduate education and for my professional development after receiving my MLS can be summarized as diversifying my work experience, contributing to a national discourse about important topics in the field, such as rethinking our classification systems and recognizing their implicit bias, and getting to know my peers in LIS. Since I've only worked within an academic library setting, I want to learn more about how I can service my community at large, not just students and faculty, according to the best practices and core values in the field. Likewise, I think attending, and possibly participating in conferences will keep me in touch with emerging trends in archives. The list below summarizes my most immediate goals for getting involved in LIS.

  • Find employment in an archive
  • Become an SAA and ALA member
  • Attend national conferences
  • Contribute to ongoing discussions in the field
  • Stay informed of continuing education opportunities
