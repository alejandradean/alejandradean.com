M.S. in Archives Management

September 2015 - Present

At present I am enrolled in the Simmons School of Library and Information Science in the archives management concentration. As mentioned in my bio, my decision to apply to Simmons after graduating from Harvard was informed by my time spent working as a circulation desk and reading room assistant at Harvard's Harry Elkins Widener Memorial Library and by my experience at the Griffith Institute at Oxford. Simmons has really broadened my perspective on the archival field, what it means to be an archivist, what the emerging trends are in the field (and in LIS in general), and what work needs to be done to rethink how collections have been organized, presented, and represented in the past. I feel so lucky to have the opportunity to learn under some of the leading faculty in the profession. Speaking of inspirational faculty, my academic hero is Laurel Thatcher Ulrich, professor of History at Harvard, whose writings primarily focus on exploring our material culture. I am currently reading her book, Tangible Things: Making History Through Objects. Ulrich's way of story-telling through collections additionally inspired me to become an archivist.
