The Fitchburg Line Industrial Corridor

I came across The Fitchburg Line Industrial Corridor page while researching what I can only describe as a giant pink wall in my neighborhood. My friends and I had been collectively mystified by this wall, which spans what seems like multiple blocks along Dane Ave. and beyond...until I discovered that it wasn't a wall at all ("that's no moon!"), but a huge building! Turns out it's the L. Bornstein Flooring Co. warehouse. A big thank you to whoever published this page, which as far as I know is THE ONLY resource that's identified this edifice online. If you live in Union Square, check out the other buildings the author writes about...it's a complete history of why there are so many warehouses in our neighborhood!

Super Nova Deja Vu

This NY Times article discusses a particularly fascinating phenomenon that was visible in our night sky last year. Astronomers using the Hubble Telescope discovered they were watching the same star explode over and over again because of the way light travels in our universe. The author writes, "light rays from the star have been bent and magnified by the gravity of an intervening cluster of galaxies so that multiple images of it appear" over the years. So cool!!
