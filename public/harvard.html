B.A. in History of Art and Architecture

September 2009 - May 2013

I graduated from Harvard in 2013 with a B.A. in History of Art and Architecture focusing on modern and contemporary art. I wrote my thesis on British land artist, Richard Long, and discussed the intersection of history and memory in his documentative work. My secondary focus in HAA was on Japanese Edo-period woodblock prints, an interest that I continue to study outside of my graduate studies in archives management at Simmons. While at Harvard, I lived in Kirkland House, DJed on the Record Hospital for WHRB, Harvard Radio Broadcasting, for four years, and was a member of the Design Board for the Harvard Advocate (Harvard's student-run literary magazine). You can actually see the cover I designed for the magazine's 2013 Commencement issue here. My biggest regret from my undergraduate career is neglecting to study a language. One of my personal goals is to become trilingual and speak English, Spanish, and Japanese fluently. I would also like to be able to read Japanese writings from the 18th and 19th centuries without recourse to translations.
